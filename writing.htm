Math is Romantic

Shall I compare thee to the whole of math?
Thou art more comely and much more complex:
though proofs can ask more wit than this one hath
thou need’st my P like NP ‘fore all sex.
Of course, hard work makes sweeter “QED” --
but coyness as thine property preferr’d?
From my ilk, it makes an enemy,
for incompleteness recalls maths absurd:
not smooth, discrete; not real, rational! Or
perhaps, you are complex, with one part real
and one imagined. Then, I do implore
you: go the distance -- accept my zeal!
What’s that? Your loins, by math, are left quite dry?
Then, leave and math will complete me! Good-bye!

A Temporary Cessation of Breath

It feels like choking. No one ever said
that heat could be so cruel, when hung like wool-
en covers on me, rife with moisture bled
from me. And every breath is like a pull
that’s harsh as hell and from a rotten cig-
arette. And every breath makes me diffuse
like smoke that trails a burnt-up fag. I’m wigg-
ing out: the fun is done, we drank the booze,
and all that’s left is viscous, naked heat.
But no one told me heat could be so cruel,
like a slow burn of all things warm and sweet.
I’m still unsure, but not of you. You look cool,
so cool, you warm summer’s night. Would you stay
if I compared you to a summer’s day?
